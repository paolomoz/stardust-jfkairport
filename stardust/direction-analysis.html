Two custom-licensed type families: Sharp Grotesk Semibold (Lucas Sharp / Sharp Type — the same family Adobe and several Pentagram clients use) for display, and Helvetica Now for PANYNJ (Monotype private cut) for body. Execution under-cashes the investment

The hero says "global brand"; everything below it reverts to a 2019-era municipal template — full-bleed photo → 52px h2 → 3-column icon-card grid → tinted alt section → repeat. The same skeleton on home, flights, transportation, explore-jfk, and dine-shop-relax. The site is recognisable as a template, not as JFK. The 320px hero gets the budget; everything beneath it gets the framework.

02Six load-bearing tensions

Detected by rule-based scans against the per-page JSON and the consolidated brand-extraction. Each one is specific enough to brief a fix against.

T-color-imbalance

One hue carries every affordance

#0045FF appears 211 times across the sample, used identically for plain link text, primary CTAs, active tabs, and headline accents. When one color does every job, hierarchy collapses — users can't tell "navigate" from "submit" at a glance.

T-display-body-cliff

Heroic display, conventional body

The 320px Sharp Grotesk hero is exceptional. The very next viewport collapses to a generic 16/20px Helvetica Now grid that any 2019 municipal site could produce. The type investment is being under-cashed below the fold.

T-template-silhouette

Same composition on every IA pillar

Full-bleed photo → "Welcome / Discover / Get / Find" h2 → 3-column icon-card grid → cross-promo → footer. Five pages, identical skeleton. Recognisable as municipal-modernism, not as JFK.

T-audience-collision

Five audiences, one undifferentiated home

A panicked late traveller, a relaxed early arrival, a pickup driver during construction, a connecting passenger, and a dwell-time visitor share the same first viewport. Audience routing is implicit, not explicit.

T-voice-distance

No human warmth in a high-anxiety context

Airports induce anxiety. The voice is uniformly procedural — "Welcome to John F. Kennedy International Airport," "Get your essentials and more at JFK." No NYC flavour, no acknowledgment of stress. Compare with Singapore Changi (theatrical) or Heathrow (concierge).

T-perf-and-a11y

Editorial images 12× oversampled, alt-text mostly empty

Editorial card images are served at 1080×1440 intrinsic but rendered at 312×416 — ~12× oversampled by area. Nine of ten sampled hero/card images have empty alt. Real perf cost on mobile; meaningful accessibility gap.

03How peer airports solve this

A landscape pass across the airports JFK gets compared to. Each one stakes a different posture — there's no "right" answer, but there are coherent answers and incoherent ones.

Where JFK currently sits

Closest to Schiphol minus the calm — same utility-led ambition, but cluttered with colliding audiences, a single-hue affordance system, and a template silhouette that doesn't match the type investment. The brand surface implies a more confident posture than the page composition delivers.

04Five directions worth weighing

Each direction is internally coherent. They imply different trade-offs in cost, risk, and PANYNJ buy-in. Recommendation flagged on the right.

A Civic-confident refresh Risk lowRecommended baseline

"The brand was right, the execution wasn't."

Keep Sharp Grotesk + Helvetica Now + the PANYNJ blue family. Fix the six tensions without rewriting the brand. Differentiate the affordance system (CTA fill ≠ link text ≠ active tab). Cash in the display family below the fold (editorial layouts on transportation and explore). Add explicit audience routing on the home page. Ship perf and alt-text fixes.

05The read

An honest convergence of what the data, the peer landscape, and PANYNJ's positioning all point toward.

Recommendation: A or B, with C layered onto whichever wins.

A (civic-confident refresh) is the safe presales answer — same brand surface, fixed execution. Brand team will recognise themselves immediately.

B (NYC-flavoured) is the more interesting answer but needs PANYNJ buy-in on warmth and on letting an editorial register into a civic site. If buy-in is plausible, B is more durable.

C (anxiety-first, journey-staged) is an IA decision that can be made independently of the visual direction. Either A or B is improved by adopting C.

D and E are real options but have clear failure modes. D ages out at the end of redevelopment. E is off-strategy for a public agency and would need an explicit rebrand mandate.

Two questions to converge before we brief the spec

  1. Direction. A (civic refresh) vs B (NYC-flavoured) vs B+C (NYC-flavoured + journey-staged IA)?
  2. Mode. Should we keep the Sharp Grotesk + Helvetica Now + PANYNJ blue brand surface (recommended; signal is strong), or are you open to a partial palette/type move?

What happens after we agree

Once direction lands, the next step is stardust:direct — it produces a target PRODUCT.md, target DESIGN.md, a direction.md reasoning trace, and a per-page improvements list. From there stardust:prototype renders one or more prototype pages we can review before committing to a full migration.
